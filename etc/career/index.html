Programming

(Relevant link: my GitHub profile.)

Since May 2022, I've been working as a Software Developer at the Distributed Digital Music Archives and Libraries (DDMAL) Lab at McGill University. The lab makes tools for musicologists; my work there has been to develop and maintain CANTUS Database. CantusDB is built in Django/Python. I've been lead developer on the project since September 2022.

From May to August 2021, I was a participant at The Recurse Center, a self-directed retreat where I developed my programming and Python abilities while working on projects with other program participants. While I was there, I helped to build a tool for exploring the interlocking patterns found in Balinese Gamelan music, and helped to train a neural network to generate melodies in the style of traditional Irish dance tunes.

I think of myself as a mostly self-taught programmer. I learned a bit of HTML in elementary school an took a couple of programming courses in high school, but otherwise, I learned to program in Python by doing tutorials and online courses, reading books, and working on personal projects. These personal projects include this website as well as a couple of early efforts to create Python libraries to explore topics in music and music theory.

Since December 2022, I've also done a little bit of freelance web development. Most of it has been simple HTML, but I've done a bit of work with JavaScript too.

Music

I've spent a bunch of time thinking about and making music.

  • In 2022, I completed an MA in Music Theory at McGill University.
  • In 2017, I completed a BA in Music at the University of Waterloo.
  • Lately, living in Montreal, I've spent a lot of time learning and playing trad music (i.e., "fiddle music").
    • I've played at sessions, mostly Québecois and Irish sessions, and mostly on fiddle and tin whistle.
    • I've played music for contradances, mostly on fiddle, mandolin and guitar.
    • I've busked, mostly on fiddle, mandolin and guitar.
  • Before the pandemic in Montreal, I participated in several musical groups in addition to busking and playing at sessions:
    • I sang with the Orpheus Singers.
    • I rehearsed with Gamelan Giri Ketadon at the Univerity of Montreal.
  • During my undergrad at the University of Waterloo, I participated in many musical activities:
    • During successive summer terms, I directed the Ad Hoc (with the exemplary Evy Kassirer), the Post Hoc, and two iterations of the Propter Hoc Chamber Choir, each a pickup chamber choir. We performed at the local library, local porch parties, and at our own concerts where we raised money for local charities.
    • I sang with the University of Waterloo Chamber and University Choirs.
    • I played in the Conrad Grebel Balinese Gamelan Ensemble.
    • I played guitar and bass in the University of Waterloo Stage (i.e., Jazz) Band.
    • I served as music director at St. Joseph's Catholic Church.
    • I played guitar and bass in the house band for Hep Cat's Swing Dance Studio, where I also facilitated weekly open jazz jam sessions.
