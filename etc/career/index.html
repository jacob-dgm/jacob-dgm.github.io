I’m Looking for a Job

I’m looking for a new job. This page is meant to be an informal summary of what I can do, what I have done, and what I’d like to do. It’s currently under construction!

For a somewhat more formal summary of what I have done, a link to my “everything CV” will soon appear here, which includes all the things I might consider putting on a resumé and which would be distilled for any specific position for which I might apply.

Up-front tl;dr

I’m looking for job. I’d like to find a position either as a programmer, or as something else. I’ve spent the past year working as a web developer, using mostly Python and Django. I have a B.A. in Music with a minor in Geography, and an M.A. in Music Theory.

Ideally, I’d like to find a position where I can make the world a better place while making a living. I can imagine myself being satisfied doing something related to either education or the environment.

Motivation

There are a bunch of good things about my current job (I’m working as a web developer for the Distributed Digital Music Archives and Libraries Lab (DDMAL), at McGill University, where we make tools for musicologists), but I’d like to find something new for a few reasons. Personally, I’m not interested in spending multiple years of my adult life working as a programmer in academia. And from a big picture point of view, it would be ideal if the lab’s grant money, currently going to pay my salary, could instead go towards funding a current student.

What I’ve done

  • Over the past year or so at DDMAL, I worked to reverse-engineer Cantus Database (it used to run in Drupal, but we’ve rebuilt it in Django). I’ve been leading this project for the past 8 months or so.
  • For my Master’s degree, I wrote a thesis on the Canadian fiddle player Oliver Schroer. I can research. I can write. I can communicate clearly.
  • As part of my project of learning to code over the past couple of years, I bought a subscription to Codecademy and worked through a bunch of the courses there. I don’t place an enormous amount of stock in the courses I completed there - I think my unstructured explorations at the Recurse Center and the work I’ve done at DDMAL are much more valuable than the step-by-step courses on Codecademy. But hey, they gave me a bunch of certificates which I may as well put somewhere. And that may as well be here, even if only to give a general sense of the languages and concepts I’m familiar with.
  • Over the past several years, I have also worked as
    • a choir director
    • a music teacher, teaching violin, mandolin, guitar and beginner piano
    • an interpreter in a provincial park
    • a teaching assistant

What I’d like to do

I’ve learned that roughly 25 hours per week is a healthy and sustainable amount of work for me, so I’d like to find a job where I can take on such an almost-full-time workload.

I’ve spent a bunch of time reading and thinking about how I can learn more effectively, and by extension how to teach well. With this in mind, I think I could be very happy working for a company that makes education technology products (two lighthouse companies/products for me are Duolingo and Brilliant).

I really enjoy making and listening to music, and I’ve spent many, many hours over the past few years (during which time I completed a BA in Music and a MA in Music Theory) thinking and writing about how music works. It would be great to find a position where I can make use of this musical knowledge.

I have interests in ecology and sustainability, and I would be happy if my job involved working to protect and restore the earth’s biosphere. Though it’s been a while since I’ve done any work academically in these fields, I began my undergraduate at the University of Waterloo in the Faculty of Environment, worked for a summer as a Natural Heritage Educator at a provincial park, and graduated with a minor in Geography and Environmental Management.

As far as coding is concerned, I like working in Python and am familiar with Django, but I’m also open to and interested in learning other languages (Rust? Something functional?) or frameworks if necessary.
