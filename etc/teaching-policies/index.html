Teaching Policies

It's exciting to hear you're interested in studying with me! Before your first lesson, please take a glance through the following policies and pieces of information:

Contact

If you need to reach me with something non-urgent, please email me at music@jacobdgm.com. I'll get back to you within a day or so (likely only a few hours).

If you need to reach me with something urgent or time-sensitive, please text me. You should already have my number.

If you don't already have my number, please let me know, and I'll give it to you.

Cancellations and Missed Lessons

If you need to miss a lesson, please let me know with as much advance notice as possible. If you cancel a lesson on short notice, I may request that you pay a cancellation fee.

  • If you give me 7 or more days' notice to cancel a lesson, there is no cancellation fee.
  • If you give me less than 7 days' notice but more than 24 hours, there is a cancellation fee of 50% of the cost of the lesson. If we're able to reschedule the lesson to a time within 7 days of when it was initially scheduled, this fee is waived.
  • If you give me less than 24 hours' notice, the cancellation fee is 100% of the cost of the lesson. But if we're able to reschedule the lesson to within 7 days of when it was initially scheduled, the cancellation fee is only 50%.
  • If you give me less than 1 hour's notice that you'll be missing a lesson, I expect to be paid in full for the missed lesson. (Please try not to do this!)

If something comes up causing you to miss a scheduled lesson that you think warrants an exception, let me know, and we'll work out something that's fair. Conducting a lesson over a video conferencing platform like Zoom or Google Meet is always an option if you can't make it to a lesson in person.

For my part, if something comes up such that I need to postpone/reschedule a lesson, if I gave you less than 7 days' notice, the rescheduled lesson is half-price.

Why a cancellation fee? When someone schedules a lesson with me, I set aside a block of time for them in my schedule. This is time I could have made plans to do something else, like teaching another student, playing a gig, or inviting friends over to play board games. I also spend time preparing to teach a lesson in the days and weeks leading up to it.

Lesson Duration

I always take the last 5 minutes of a lesson to get set up for a next student, take notes on what we covered, and start drafting up an email summarizing what we did and what to work on before a next lesson.

For example, if you have a 60-minute lesson scheduled at 1:00, the teaching portion of the lesson ends at 1:55. If you have a 30-minute lesson scheduled at 1:00, teaching ends at 1:25.

Rates

As of January 2025, my default rates for new students are:

  • For a single lesson: $75/hr
  • For a lesson in a series of lessons (three or more lessons in a row, say, with no more than a few weeks between successive lessons): $65/hr

I don't ever want cost to be something that stands between someone and a music lesson they'd like to take. If money is tight, please suggest a rate you can afford—I will almost certainly accept it. (And if it's within your means to pay a higher rate and you'd like to help subsidize others' lessons, do let me know!)

Payment

If you can, please pay by e-transfer to jacob@jacobdgm.com (music@jacobdgm.com is fine too). If e-transfer isn't a possibility for you, we'll find another option (likely cash or Paypal).

I'm looking forward to working together!
